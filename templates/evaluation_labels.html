Indicators of disinformation

  Emotion: {{ metrics[key][0] }}% of articles with this emotivity level are fake.

One characteristic of disinformation is that it may make an inflammatory emotional appeal to the reader, so it is important to detect the emotions that the news transmits.

  Subjectivity: {{ metrics[key][1] }}% of articles with this subjectivity level are fake.

Disinformation often contains a lot of subjective expressions. This category is responsible to distinguish factual information from subjective information. Subjective expressions are used to express an opinion, emotion, evaluation, stance, speculation by the author.

  Affectivity: {{ metrics[key][2] }}% of articles with this affectivity level are fake.

Disinformation often employs a higher number of emotional words than informative text. Affective metrics compute statistics on emotional words present in text and highlight variations to informative content.

  Polarity: {{ metrics[key][3] }}% of articles with this polarity level are fake.

Disinformation employs a lot of opinions expressed by the author. The metrics in this category deal with sentiments that are expressed through subjective expressions to describe people's opinions or feelings towards a given target.

  Behavioral & Physiological (BP): {{ metrics[key][4] }}% of articles with this BP level are fake.

In a news article, the author also shows the feelings that he had, the information that he gathered. These feelings depends on his behaviour and psychological processes and can help to detect disinformation.
